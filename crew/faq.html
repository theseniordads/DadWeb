How the faq did you get started?

Well, the story of how we came to do our amazing first demo is very well documented , but it doesn't tell you how we first came together!!!

Originally, when we were teenagers, I ( Old Fart !!!!) became friends with Doddering Git . We both had Atari STs, and Doddering Git was really into the ST Demo scene , and was showing me all these really great demos like the Onion Demo by Delta Boys and Dark Side of the Inner Circle by The Lost Carebears!!! We thought "Wouldn't it be great if we could do demoes like that?!?!? Yes!!!! Hey, let's try and make one of our own!!!!"

And even we were suprised at how good our demo was!!! So we sent it off to ST Floormat who were doing a feature on demos, and asked them what they thought of it!!! Unfortunately, they ripped us off , and it really disillousouned us!!!!

Luckily we got back into demo coding once Atari brought out the Faclon , and we each got one!!!! We developed a couple of really kick-ass demo tricks which we later used in Anal Tuck and Colonic Irrigation . At this time,we got friendly with Dame Vera Lynn , who had been in a real-life demo crew before, and was trying to fit his ample musical talents into sound tracker modules !!!!

We then went to the demo party Fried Place To Be 4 in 1995, and at that party we decided to go for it, and make and release our first ever demo, Anal Tuck !!! And it got quite a response!!!!

Shortly after we released our second demo, we decided to do a website (ie this one!!!!)!! We needed help with the website GFX, so the Dame introduced us to Jackson Pollock, the guy who was the artist in the demo crew the Dame was in, before they were both chucked out because of demo crew politics !!!!

His job on the initial 2 page SD website was so impressive, we asked him to join the crew!!! And he did!!!!!! And just before we released our amazing Air Dirt Demo , we met an Amiga coder called Colostomy Bag , who was really amazed by our demos, and wanted to code demos like that on the Amiga!!!

So that's how we came to be!!!

What the faq is the "Air demo"???!?

The Beach Boys have " Smile ", we have " The Air Demo "!!!!

The Air Demo is a demo we planned to release after Colonic Irrigation . We'd done fire and water for our first two demos, so we decided to do air and earth for the next two, starting with the Air demo ! At the time, we had got friendly with a crew called IPIR , and we were swapping tips and source and stuff. Then, while we were finishing the Air demo, IPIR released a demo at the NASA96 show with effects that looked very similar to our early Air demo effects!!!!

We feared the worst,quickly cancelled the Air demo, and worked on a completely new demo which was to become the Air Dirt demo , and stopped talking to IPIR!!! In fact, when we released Air Dirt not long afterwards, we accused IPIR of ripping our code in the README!!!

As it turned out, we were wrong- sorry IPIR!!! In fact, IPIR were just inspired by some of our demos, and coded their own versions!!!

The Air demo remains unfinished, but if there's enough demand, and we have enough time, we might possibly finish it...

What the faq is "JTS", "Hasbro", "Pacifist", etc. ?!?!?

Er, well, it really used to be simple!!!! It used to be that you could call our demos " Atari " demos, but in the past few years things hav got reely complex!!! First, Atari merged with an HD company called JTS , and were called "JTS Atari", and then the rights to the name were sold to makers of the Transformers , who are Hasbro !!! That's confusing enough, but there's also an Atari ST emulator for the PC called Pacifist , which can run some of our demos really well!!!! So it's anyone's guess now!!!! Basically all the above are words for the platform formerly known as Atari !!!!

Well, how many T-shirts do you see with the Microsoft logo on it in a hip 'n' trendy nightclub?!?!?!  We think it's a really undervalued platform, and with our brilliant multimedia experiences , we are making Atari great again!!!! Well, I think it's a good enough reason anyway!!!!!

Actually, we are also wanting to spread some of our audiovisual magic to other platforms as well, but we haven't got round to it yet!!! For a start, this PC demo coding lark looks a bit complex!!!
